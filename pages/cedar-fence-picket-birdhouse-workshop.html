Cedar Fence Picket Birdhouse Workshop

A fun family-and-friends birdhouse project made from one cedar fence picket, simple cuts, and a lot of personality.

The idea

This project turns one cedar fence picket from Home Depot into a fun birdhouse build-and-decorate activity. Everyone starts with the same basic structure, but the finished birdhouses can all end up looking completely different.

That is part of what makes this project so good. It is practical, beginner-friendly, and creative at the same time.

Why this project works

It is simple enough for a beginner, fun enough for a group project, and charming enough that the finished birdhouses feel like something people are proud to take home.

What you start with

  • 1 cedar fence picket
  • stick pieces cut to size
  • front and back gables
  • roof pieces
  • glue and/or pin nails
  • paint and decorations for finishing

Working cut notes

  • All the stick pieces are 1/2 inch pieces
  • 2 longer pieces at 6 1/2 inches, then cut into 1/2 inch sticks
  • 2 shorter pieces at 4 3/8 inches, then cut into 1/2 inch sticks
  • 2 gables at 3 3/4 inches with a 45 degree peak
  • Base cut at 8 inches
  • Roof panels cut at 8 inches
  • One roof width note shown at 6 1/2 inches

This is still a working version and should be refined further, but it already gives a solid visual starting point.

Assembly steps

1. Layout the stick pieces on the base

Start by laying the cut stick pieces on the dogear base. Glue or pin nail them in place.

2. Build the body in log cabin rows

Keep assembling one row at a time in a log-cabin pattern, overlapping every other row.

3. Add the gable pieces

Glue or pin nail the front, back, and filler gable pieces to shape the roof line.

4. Assemble the roof

Glue and pin nail the two roof pieces together as a separate little assembly.

5. Place the roof last

Place the roof last, but do not glue it on. Keeping it removable makes the birdhouse easier to clean out later.

6. Decorate it your own way

This is where the project becomes really fun. Everyone can take the same basic structure and give it a completely different personality.

What makes this project fun

Some projects are just about the finished result. This one is also about the table full of paint, the people making things together, and the way each birdhouse ends up with its own style.

That combination makes it strong for families, friends, workshops, and simple backyard project nights.
